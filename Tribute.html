Here's a time line of Muhammed Ali's life:

  • 1942- Born in Louisville, Kentucky, U.S.
  • 1960- Won a gold medal in the light heavyweight division at Summer Olympics in Rome.
  • 1964- Won the WBA, WBC and lineal heavyweight titles from Sonny Liston in an upset.
  • 1964- Between February 25, 1964, and September 19, Ali reigned as the undisputed heavyweight champion. He is the only boxer to be named The Ring magazine Fighter of the Year six times.
  • 1964- After winning first heavyweight titles, Clay then converted to Islam and changed his name from Cassius Clay, which he called his "slave name", to Muhammad Ali.
  • 1966- Two years after winning the heavyweight title, Ali further antagonized the white establishment in the U.S. by refusing to be conscripted into the U.S. military, citing his religious beliefs and opposition to American involvement in the Vietnam War.
  • 1971- He successfully appealed in the U.S. Supreme Court, which overturned his conviction. By which time he had not fought for nearly four years—losing a period of peak performance as an athlete. Ali's actions as a conscientious objector to the war made him an icon for the larger counterculture generation.
